Highest Selling Game of each Decade; 80s: Tetris

Tetris Introduction

Tetris is one of the most iconic and best-selling video games of all time, recognized across generations and cultures. Its simple design, addictive gameplay, and universal appeal have allowed it to remain relevant for more than four decades.

  • Over 520 million copies sold across platforms
  • Easy to learn, difficult to master
  • Requires no language or cultural knowledge

Origins and Early History

Tetris was created in 1984 by Russian computer scientist Alexey Pajitnov while working at the Soviet Academy of Sciences. Inspired by geometric puzzles, the game was originally designed as an experiment in artificial intelligence and player psychology.

  • Developed on an Electronika 60 computer
  • Name derived from “tetromino” and “tennis”
  • Initially spread through floppy disc sharing
  • Early versions had no sound or color

Rise to Global Phenomenon

Tetris gained worldwide popularity after being bundled with the Nintendo Game Boy in 1989. This strategic pairing transformed the game into a household name and helped define portable gaming.

  • Game Boy bundle boosted massive sales
  • Popular in both Eastern and Western markets
  • Became a competitive and speed-running title
  • Recognized by its iconic music and visuals

Impacts on Gaming Today

Tetris has had a lasting influence on modern game design, emphasizing simplicity, accessibility, and replay value. Its mechanics are still studied and replicated in game development today.

  • Popularized puzzle game mechanics
  • Inspired countless clones and adaptations
  • Used in studies on memory and cognition
  • Continues to evolve with modern versions and esports
